3.1. Scores and Scales

In this report, we have categorized the identified vulnerabilities based on their overall severity and their potential impact on business operations.

The severity of each vulnerability is evaluated using the following scale:

  • Critical: Critical-severity vulnerabilities signify an imminent and severe threat to the target system, demanding immediate corrective action or, in extreme cases, the immediate shutdown of the affected service.
  • High: High-severity vulnerabilities indicate a significant risk to the target system, requiring prompt correction to prevent or minimize potential adverse effects.
  • Medium: Moderate-severity vulnerabilities present a discernible risk to the target system, requiring timely correction in the medium term to mitigate potential consequences.
  • Low: Low-severity vulnerabilities pose minimal risk to the target system and may need corrective measures to prevent potential impacts in the future.
  • Informational: Informational findings do not represent immediate security threats. They highlight areas for improvement in best practices to enhance the overall security posture of the system.

3.2. Remediation Priority

For each vulnerability there is a priority indicator with a guideline of the remediation times in accordance with Randstad's policy:

  • Critical (P1): Within 14 days
  • High (P2): Within 30 days
  • Medium (P3): Within 45 days
  • Low (P4): Within 60 days
  • Informational (P5): Within 270 days

The Global Offensive Security Team published all vulnerabilities on the Keep It Secure 24 platform. Every severity level has its own maximum time to solve. The table below will give you an approximate due date.

The vulnerabilities need to be resolved before this due date.

When the vulnerability has been fixed, the Global Offensive Security Team will retest the fix. With this validation, the team will take a close look at the implementation and bypass techniques for the fix itself.

To make sure that the findings that are reported are being resolved within the designed time to solve window, the ISO must track the vulnerability status.

A fix may take more time to resolve due to multiple reasons. When this happens an exception for extra time must be raised via ServiceNow: Request Security Exception. Once a Security exception has been approved, you may update the vulnerability status to "Parked" in Keep It Secure 24.

If you need instructions on how to raise a security exception, please refer to Appendix 6.2. section.

6. Appendix ↑

6.1 Appendix - Disclaimer

Our analysis and conclusions, despite the expertise of our team, are subject to certain limitations:

  • The penetration test performed makes it possible to detect certain vulnerabilities. In no case they prove the absence of vulnerabilities, especially within the limited timeframe.
  • Our team operates within defined scope boundaries, unlike malicious actors who are not bound by the same limitations of scope and time.
  • This report does not predict the future evolution of the target environment and associated threats. It reflects only the security status of the systems at the time of the penetration test.

6.2. Appendix - Raising a Security Exception

Instructions for submitting a Global Security Exception:

Navigate to the designated Global Security Exception Request Form using the following link: https://randstadglobal.service-now.com/motion?id=sc_cat_item&table=sc_cat_item&sys_id=cbb8774a970159108f77be7fe153af6f.
